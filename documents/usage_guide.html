🎯 Step 1: Determine Your Generation (Gn)

First, you need to identify your generation relative to your G0 ancestor:

  • G1 = The first generation born outside Canada
  • G0 = The parents of G1 (your Canadian-connected ancestor)
  • Your Gn = How many generations you are from G0

🔍 Examples:

Example 1: Your great-grandparent was born outside Canada → They are G1 → Their parents are G0 → You are G4

Example 2: Your 2x great-grandparent was born outside Canada → They are G1 → Their parents are G0 → You are G5

Generation definitions from: Canadian Citizenship Act Changes - Rules 2025

✏️ Step 3: Complete Your PDF

Your chosen PDF contains editable form fields:

  • Text Fields: Click and type information about your ancestors
  • Radio Buttons: Select "Yes" or "No" for citizenship questions
  • Multi-line Fields: Use Enter key for new lines in larger text areas

💡 Pro Tips:

  • Save your PDF after filling it out
  • Print on 8.5" x 11" paper for best results
  • Submit with your main CIT 0001 application
